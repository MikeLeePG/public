What We Mean by Integration

Most organizations don't have a data problem — they have a connection problem. Information lives in a dozen places: a club management platform here, a student information system there, spreadsheets emailed back and forth, PDFs buried in shared drives. Each system works on its own, but none of them talk to each other. The result is that the people who need answers spend their time hunting for data instead of acting on it.

At Proof, integration means becoming what we call "human middleware." We sit between your systems, your processes, and your people — understanding how work actually flows before we connect a single data point. We've mapped a 15-system landscape for a college prep school, unified years of scattered financial documents for a private club, and built middleware layers that keep schools running while legacy platforms are carefully retired. The technology matters, but it's never where we start.

The payoff is straightforward: when your systems finally communicate, your staff stop being data couriers and start being decision-makers. The admissions director who used to spend mornings copying records between platforms now reviews a single dashboard. The operations manager who cobbled together reports from three sources now asks a question in plain language and gets an answer in seconds. Integration gives your people their expertise back.

AI Integration

Giving databases a conversational layer — with guardrails

ProofChat is Proof's own AI-driven interface that lets users interact with Claris FileMaker data using natural language. Instead of navigating complex screens or writing queries, users ask questions in plain language — and the system translates those into real actions: pulling reports, answering questions, even updating records.

Critically, ProofChat is built with a human-in-the-loop philosophy. Sensitive operations require manual approval before execution. The AI's actions stay grounded in actual data, not hallucinated answers. It's a model for how integration with AI should work: powerful access, human oversight.

How We Work

We listen before we build

Before writing a line of code, we map your real processes — how data actually moves, who touches it, and where things break down. The architecture comes from understanding, not assumptions.

When a 1,700-student school in California asked for help, we started by mapping all 15 of their interconnected systems — campus ministry, financial aid, registration, and more — before proposing a single change. That map became the foundation for everything that followed.

We preserve your investment

You don't have to throw away what you've built. We connect your existing systems, strengthen what's working, and retire what isn't — without losing the data and institutional knowledge you've accumulated.

A private club in the Mountain West had years of financial and membership data scattered across formats and platforms. Instead of starting over, we integrated those documents with their existing management software — preserving every data point and making it all queryable for the first time.

We iterate, not overhaul

Big-bang launches are risky and stressful. We deliver in focused phases — each one solving a real problem and proving value — so you see progress early and can adjust course as you learn.

A K-8 school in Manhattan needed to modernize nearly every operational system. Rather than one massive rollout, we worked phase by phase — admissions first, then enrollment, then after-school programming — each stage building on the last. The school never had to stop and wait.

We stay human-in-the-middle

Automation is powerful, but oversight matters. We design systems where people stay in the loop — especially when actions affect real data, real money, or real people.

ProofChat lets users query and update a database using natural language. But any operation that modifies data requires a human to approve it first. We built it that way on purpose — because the goal of integration is to empower people, not replace their judgment.

A Note on Technology

We work across a wide range of platforms and tools — Claris FileMaker, Python, Django, REST APIs, AI and large language models, cloud infrastructure, single sign-on, workflow automation, and more. If your systems involve it, chances are we've connected to it.

But we've learned that the technology is rarely the hard part. The real work is understanding your processes — how work actually flows through your organization, who uses the systems, who avoids them and why, and what becomes possible when everything finally connects. Proof was founded by educators, not engineers. That background shows up in how we approach every project: people and processes first, technology in service of both.
